Art Gallery Project

Hereum Gallery

Yeonhui-dong, Seoul, Korea



2020

Architecture Studio 3



Entrance plaza (1F)’s stairs can be a means of transportation, but also allow people to sit down and rest. The entrance plaza is designed to have a variety of possibilities, including vertical interaction with the upper floors and horizontal interaction with transparent glass façades. The flow of the stairs of the entrance square continues inside the gallery as well. The scenery of the city is at the left, and the houses are at the front.







Interior Perspective



One important feature of the gallery was to design ramps so that they could come as part of the exhibition. Therefore, it was designed to connect ramps and exhibition spaces without direct walls or corridors. Since the height of the floor is constant, climbing the ramp feels as if the space is narrowing. Then, as you face an exhibition room where the height of the floor suddenly rises, you will be given an experience of compression and expansion. The polycarbonate exterior is exposed so that light could leak in softly.

The ramp acts as another exhibition. One ramp is attached to the void, so you can look down at the entrance square as you move. Another lamp has a very high floor height and a small width, which creates a very long, narrow space which allows the beautiful and subtle feeling of polycarbonate to be fully conveyed.
