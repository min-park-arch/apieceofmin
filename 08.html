Art Gallery Project

Hereum Gallery

Yeonhui-dong, Seoul, Korea



2020

Architecture Studio 3
































Site Analysis

First, the site is located at the very boundary between the residential area of Yeonhui-dong and Yonsei University campus. Therefore, if there was an art gallery on this site, it would be better to set targets as citizens living in houses or students using campuses, rather than just focusing on the role of exhibition for museum visitors.

Second, people can approach the site from the south side by either walking from the main road or coming down the mountain road near Yonsei University's Samsung Hall. Therefore, considering the direction of entry, an open design facing the south was required.

Third, it was important to note that there was height difference on the boundary of the site. If the terrain was cut into sections, the terrain height difference about 7-8m.



Mass Development










The concept of Hereum Gallery is that it has an â€˜oblique flow'. The passage was carried out according to the originally slanted terrain, and the concept was strengthened by adding diagonal movement not only in external movements but also in internal movements.

To serve as a passageway, a large hole was made first and the entry direction was adjusted to the south, consequently making the building itself be open to the public. Then, the hole was slanted to conform with the flow of the terrain. To further highlight this mass flow and ensure openness in the entry plaza, glass was used near the main entrance on the first floor for transparency and piloted for structural solutions.
